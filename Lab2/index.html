Cleveland, Ohio, nicknamed "The Forest City" is the seat of Cuyahoga County and is ranked as the 32nd Largest Metropolitan Area in the US.

Cleveland has a rich history and many great things to offer, including a bustling food scene, numerous venues in which to appreciate the arts, and many sports to enjoy.

Cleveland has a population of approximately 380,000 residents, while the Greater Clevelad Area consists of just over 2,000,000 people.

Cleveland was founded in 1796 by European settlers near the mouth of the Cuyahoga River. Because of its proximity to the river, Cleveland naturally became a manufacturing hub. The Cuyahoga River famously caught on fire more than a dozen times between the late nineteenth century and 1969. The last fire, in June of 1969, spurred a national environmental movement, culminating in the passage of the Clean Water Act in 1972.

Cleveland was named after Moses Cleaveland and was incorporated as a city in 1836.

According to the 2010 Census, Cleveland is comprised of approximately 53% African American residents, 37% White, 0.3% Native American, 1.8% Asian, 10% Latino, and the remainder being other races or individuals of more than one race.

Some famous people from Cleveland include Steve Harvey, Bob Hope, Dr. Oz, Arsenio Hall, Drew Carey, Halle Berry, and Terrence Howard.
